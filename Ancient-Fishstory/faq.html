Fishstory FAQ

What is a Roguelike?

Traditionally a Roguelike is a turn-based game with an ascii (text-based, imagine your character is an @ that moves around the screen) or graphical tile-based world that has RPG or adventure elements.

The learning and difficulty curve is often steep, and can involve perma-death (where you have a single life, and have to start from the beginning when you die).

Is this one of those fake Roguelikes?

The Roguelike genre has evolved over time to include many offshoots in gameplay style and form. Many games will use the genre "Rogue-lite" to signify looser ties to more traditional titles of the genre.

This game has many aspects of traditional roguelikes, in that it is turn-based, and that the default difficulty involves permadeath.

One major difference is that this game uses 3d cube coordinates instead of 2d tiles, and displays the game in two views, top-down, and side-view.

What inspired this game?

This game pulls much of it's influence from other classic roguelikes and dungeon crawlers.
Stylistically it draws from some classic 90's console games such as EVO and Ecco The Dolphin.
